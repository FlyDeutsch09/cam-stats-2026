### Selection Criteria (User-Specified)

1. **Chinese-friendly** — Good track record with Chinese mainland applicants
2. **Not too small** — Reasonable size, healthy community
3. **Stable admissions** — No sudden significant increase in admissions (volatility concern)
4. **Not too many Winter Pool pickups** — Prefers colleges that make direct offers
5. **Reasonable offer rate** — Not excessively competitive
6. **Reasonable PG (post-graduate/conditional offer) requirements**

## Cambridge Engineering Department Overview

### Course Structure
- **Duration:** 4 years (MEng)
- **Years 1-2:** Broad engineering foundation — mechanical, structural, electrical, information, materials
- **Years 3-4:** Specialisation in one of ~10 areas (Aerospace, Civil, Electrical, Mechanical, etc.)
- **Teaching:** Department-based lectures + college-based supervisions (small group teaching)

### Entry Requirements
- **A-Level:** A\*A\*A (Mathematics and Physics required, A\* in Mathematics strongly recommended)
- **IB:** 41-42 points with 7,7,6 at Higher Level (including Mathematics AA HL and Physics HL)
- **Admissions Test:** ESAT (Engineering and Science Admissions Test) — replaced ENGAA from 2025 cycle
- **Interview:** College-based, typically 2 interviews

### Key Statistics (2025 Cycle)
- **3,263 applicants** → **393 offers** → **~335 acceptances**
- **Offer rate: 12.0%** (one of the most competitive courses at Cambridge)
- **China mainland:** 599 applicants, ~66 offers (11% offer rate)
- **2026 trend:** Applications up to 3,972, offer rate dropping to ~10.4%

## Eligible Colleges — Detailed Profiles

### Fitzwilliam College
- **Size:** Large (~500 undergrads), diverse community
- **Location:** North-west Cambridge, 10-min walk to Engineering Department
- **Engineering reputation:** Very strong — large engineering cohort, dedicated Engineering Fellows
- **Chinese friendliness:** HIGH — consistently good Chinese student numbers, active Chinese society
- **Winter Pool behavior:** Both a pooler (sends applicants to pool) and acceptor (takes from pool) — balanced
- **Offer rate (all subjects):** ~18.5%
- **Note:** Fastest-growing in applications (+20.31% in 2025) — this could be a concern for the user's "stability" criterion
- **IB typical offer:** 41-42 with 7,7,6 at HL

### Homerton College

- **Location:** South Cambridge (Hills Road), ~15-min bike to Engineering Department

## Key Observations

1. **Chinese-friendly colleges** (Robinson, Homerton, Fitzwilliam) consistently show higher Chinese student numbers and better support networks for international students.

2. **Winter Pool behavior varies significantly:**
   - **Heavy poolers** (Emmanuel, Trinity, St John's) — send many applicants to the pool, meaning even strong candidates may not get direct offers
   - **Low poolers** (Clare, Jesus, Corpus Christi) — make more direct offers
   - **Strong acceptors** (Fitzwilliam, Homerton, Churchill) — take good candidates from the pool

3. **Stability concern (2025→2026):** Fitzwilliam saw +20.31% application growth in 2025 — the largest increase. This suggests volatility. Homerton and Robinson showed more stable growth patterns.

4. **Engineering competition by tier:**
   - **Extreme:** Trinity, Churchill, St John's (150-200+ engineering applicants)
   - **High:** Caius, Fitzwilliam, Homerton (120-140 engineering applicants)
   - **Moderate:** Emmanuel, Pembroke, Queens', Clare (90-110)
   - **Lower:** Robinson, Sidney Sussex, Selwyn, Trinity Hall, St Catharine's, Girton (60-80)
